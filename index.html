About

Falling for Annie came together over music, love, friendship, and food. A serendipitous encounter with a sailor, and his boat named Annie, inspired the trio to share their music with others. Falling for Annie is about falling for inspiration, passion, empowerment, and love… simply because it is why we are here.

Falling for Annie’s contemporary folk style consists of vibrant vocal harmonies. While many songs are a cappella, the trio also includes multiple instruments in their arrangements.

Falling for Annie includes three music teachers:Katy Lushman (soprano, uke, piano), Ally Olson (mezzo, guitar, uke, piano), and Karrisa Swanson (alto, cello, banjo, piano, uke).

Karissa Swanson

Karissa Swanson’s childhood involved long road trips throughout the Midwest singing for hours in four-part harmony with her family. She studied classical piano and cello throughout her childhood.

Her music studies continued into St. Olaf College where she toured with the St. Olaf Choir and performed with many other ensembles. Karissa is a founding member of Falling for Annie and also performs with Denver’s St. Martin’s Chamber Choir.

Karissa is an elementary music teacher in Denver Public Schools, where she works with over 400 students in grades K-5. Her students like to sing, dance, create, play, and listen to music.

Karissa lives near Denver, CO with her soulmate, Nathan. When not in Denver, they are traveling the world in a VW campervan or at sea in a liveaboard sailboat.

Katy Lushman

Katy has been a musician for as long as she can remember and it seems she’s always had a tune in her head and a song on her lips. Her parents are to thank for encouraging her take piano lessons starting at the age of five.

Trained as a singer, educator, and conductor first at the University of Cincinnati College-Conservatory of Music, Katy moved to Denver in 2013 after completing a master’s degree at Westminster Choir College. Since then, she has sung with the Anima Chamber Ensemble, Evans Choir, and the St. Martin’s Chamber Choir. Being a founding member of Falling for Annie ensures that Katy gets to regularly laugh, eat, and sing with her dear friends Karissa and Ally.

Katy has proudly taught at Denver North High School in Denver Public Schools for the last five years and enjoys daily serving her students through both music and herbal tea. She is also the co-founder of Vocal COalition, an organization that pairs teenagers with local music artists for collaborative performances. In the summer, she spends six weeks in rural western Kansas as Festival Director for the Ad Astra Music Festival, where she helps facilitate a month-long classical music series.

When she's not singing, Katy practices yoga and rides mountain bikes under the big skies of Colorado with her husband, Andy, and their pup, Sky. She agrees with Mary Oliver, of all the sights she loves in this world — and there are plenty — very near the top of the list is this one: dogs without leashes.
